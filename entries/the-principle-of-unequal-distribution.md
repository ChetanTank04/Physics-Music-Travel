<p>"The top one percent have as loot as the bottom 50 percent and where the richest eighty five people have as much as the bottom three and a half billion. That same brutal principle of unequal distribution applies outside the financial domain indeed, anywhere that creative production is required.The majority of scientific papers are published by a very small group of a scientists. A tiny proportion of musicians produces almost all the recorded commercial music. Just a handful of authors sell all the books. A million and a half separately titled books sell each year in US. However, only five hundred of thees sell more than a hundred thousand copies. Similarly just four classical composers ( Bach, Beethoven, Mozart, and Tchaikovsky) wrote almost the music played by modern orchestras. "\
A whole different take on the socialist argument of equality. I liked how he compares financial distribution to creative capabilities. And I deeply believe that establishing and running a company or a financial institution is a creative exponent. Even we can argue that there weren&rsquo;t enough women involved in science and mathematics or even in music, and that&rsquo;s why it is patriarchal. Again it comes to the same argument of unequal distribution. Very interesting hypothesis. Loved it.</p>
